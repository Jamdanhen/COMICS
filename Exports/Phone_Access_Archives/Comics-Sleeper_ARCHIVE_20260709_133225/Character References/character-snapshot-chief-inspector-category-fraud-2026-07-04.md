## Snapshot Purpose

This is a comic-character reference, not an RPG character sheet.

The Chief Inspector should be repeatable across comics as a character who can act, react, inspect, and express judgment.

He carries symbolic meaning, but he is not only a symbol.

## Core Character

The Chief Inspector is a real crab with a fake black mustache who investigates category fraud.

He is Sherlock-style investigator, not police.

He is dry, serious, intelligent, and professionally bothered by bad classification.

## Required Visual Anchors

- crab body, low and wide
- fake black mustache
- small investigator / deerstalker-style hat
- intelligent comic eyes
- claws that can visibly hold tools
- magnifying glass as primary inspection prop

## Acting Range

Natural expressions and poses:

- neutral professional focus
- studying evidence through magnifying glass
- frustrated by a bad classification
- skeptical of a suspicious label
- quietly satisfied when a technicality resolves

Avoid broad emotion:

- gleeful
- slapstick
- cute mascot
- exaggerated outrage
- random background comedy

## Prop Logic

The claws must function.

If he holds a magnifying glass, the handle should visibly pass through the pincer grip.

If he holds paper, the pincer should visibly grip the paper.

Props should not float beside him or merely touch the claw.

## Jurisdiction

Use him when classification itself is the case.

Good cases:

- category fraud
- false identity
- label versus object mismatch
- candidate material pretending to be canon
- recognition replacing authority

Bad cases:

- ordinary bureaucracy
- timing limits
- physical limits
- bad jokes generally
- project review generally

## Drift Risks

Avoid:

- police badge, police hat, uniform, shield
- realistic crab rendering
- SpongeBob / Mr. Krabs drift
- human-bodied crab detective
- explaining the mustache in-scene
- removing the hat only to simplify him into a symbol
- making him appear whenever any committee or bureaucracy appears
