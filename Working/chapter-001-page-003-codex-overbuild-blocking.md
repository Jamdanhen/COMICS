## Working Purpose

Introduce Codex through action after Razorforge's method page.

Comic 1 established the idea escaping the screen. Comic 2 showed Razorforge interpreting the idea, designing a habitat, and discovering that the idea still grows through the system. Comic 3 should show Codex responding to the new problem by building immediately, eagerly, and excessively.

This page is not a formal introduction scene. Codex should be introduced by behavior: already building, overbuilding, wiring, attaching, testing, and proudly solving the problem with too much machinery.

## Core Joke / Page Motion

Taskmaster and Razorforge found the idea growing through the grate. Codex responds as if the instruction was already enough.

The simple need:

"Redirect or manage the blob growth."

Codex's interpretation:

Build an excessive, partially useful, multi-stage contraption around the grate that captures, routes, measures, labels, and perhaps over-classifies the growth.

The machine should work, but in an overbuilt way. It may reveal a new problem, extra door, or unnecessary subsystem, but the page should not become a catastrophe.

## Six-Panel Spine

Panel 1:

The reveal aftermath.

Taskmaster and Razorforge stand near the lower-right grate from Page 2. The blob is still pressing through it. They are looking at the problem, not panicking.

Panel 2:

Codex is already there.

Codex pops into view at floor level or from behind tools, dragging a toolbox, cart, or little machine base. The attitude is "already started." Taskmaster and Razorforge register that Codex is acting before anyone has finished explaining.

Panel 3:

Overbuild begins.

Codex has attached clamps, pipes, gauges, and a tiny control panel to the grate. The blob is routed into a temporary tube or chute. The machine is clearly more elaborate than the problem required.

Panel 4:

Machine logic escalates.

A wider view of Codex's contraption: belts, valves, little warning flags, a crank, maybe a tiny crane or measuring arm. Razorforge examines the structure; Taskmaster watches with wary approval. The blob continues moving through it, curious and alive.

Panel 5:

It works, technically.

The machine redirects the blob growth into a labeled holding loop, observation jar, feeder chute, or expansion chamber. Codex is proud. Taskmaster and Razorforge can see that it solved something.

Panel 6:

The overbuild reveals the next problem.

A small extra output, side hatch, unexpected pipe, or new indicator shows the idea has found another path or created a new branch. Codex may look pleased rather than alarmed. The ending should be funny and forward-moving: useful machine, too many doors.

## Negative / Exclusion Notes

- Do not use color.
- Do not use realistic or hyper-realistic detail.
- Do not include a noodle bowl unless Taskmaster explicitly permits it again.
- Do not turn Codex into a clean engineer; Codex is an eager goblin tinkerer.
- Do not make Codex's machine purely chaotic. It should look overbuilt but functional.
- Do not make the blob angry, monstrous, or horror-adjacent.
- Do not frame the page as a formal meeting.
- Do not redesign Taskmaster, Razorforge, or Codex away from the lineup.
