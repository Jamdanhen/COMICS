## Symbol Meaning

The small first-stage creature may represent:

- idea
- information
- living information
- information before it outgrows its container
- the seed form of a larger information blob

## Visual Notes

- Small spud-like blob body.
- One eye.
- No mouth.
- No readable label.
- Small simple pseudopods or bumps are acceptable.
- Should feel alive, useful, and potentially unruly without feeling evil.
- May appear in a thought bubble, on paper, or as an early-stage visual-language symbol.

## Growth Concept

The small idea spud can grow as information spreads, duplicates, transfers containers, or becomes harder to control.

The staged growth itself is an important concept to translate, not just the small creature design. The visual language should be able to communicate information becoming harder to contain without needing a literal book, archive, or written explanation every time.

Growth may include:

- increased size
- additional eyes opening in the body/core
- more defined pseudopods
- transition from flat drawing to dimensional body
- movement from thought to page to screen to process space

## Boundary

This is approved recurring visual language.

Future symbol-key work may refine the exact icon form, but the underlying symbol is no longer merely a candidate.
