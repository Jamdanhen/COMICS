## Around Here Chronicle

Role:

The newspaper/reporting institution of Around Here.

Symbol direction:

An ordinary black-and-white newspaper front page, treated seriously. It should look like a local paper, not a fantasy tome.

Possible visual anchors:

- masthead: `Around Here Chronicle`
- folded newspaper
- ordinary columns
- one strange headline or image box
- small map-pin / Around Here sign motif if needed

Avoid:

- making it magical
- making it too modern-digital
- making the paper itself the whole mythology

## Monocle

Role:

A focused Chronicle article/lens: opinion, analysis, feature, close-up, or focused treatment.

Origin / joke:

`Monocle` rhymes with `Chronicle`.

The Monocle focuses on one thing. The Chronicle reports broadly; the Monocle zooms in.

Symbol direction:

Not just a floating monocle as generic interpretation. Better: a single newspaper clipping or article column seen through a monocle/magnifier.

Possible visual anchors:

- clipped article under a monocle
- magnified headline fragment
- focus circle around one paragraph
- small Chronicle masthead corner visible

Avoid:

- making it only a detective monocle
- losing the Chronicle article connection
- making it too Victorian unless that tone becomes useful
- losing the rhyme joke with Chronicle

## Gazelle

Role:

Anthropomorphic gazelle reporter for the Around Here Chronicle.

Origin / joke:

The Gazelle comes from the abandoned first name `Around Here Gazette`.

The word drift / misspelling from `Gazette` to `Gazelle` became a literal gazelle reporter.

Symbol direction:

A simple repeatable reporter figure: gazelle head/ears/horns, press hat or reporter notebook, microphone, satchel, or press badge.

Possible visual anchors:

- gazelle reporter holding notebook
- press card in hat band
- field reporting posture
- one hoof/hand raised with pencil

Avoid:

- realistic animal rendering
- generic mascot cheerfulness
- making the Gazelle a random animal rather than a working reporter
- losing the Gazette-to-Gazelle origin

## Biological Committee

Role:

Committee authority over sleep, hunger, time, physical limits, and other barriers to project work or idea creation.

Symbol direction:

The committee may be represented through official notices, violation slips, reminders, stamps, or citations rather than a creature mascot.

Possible visual anchors:

- official violation notice
- reminder slip
- clipboard with checkboxes: sleep, hunger, time, physical limit
- stamp reading `VIOLATION` or `REMINDER`
- clock, stomach icon, pillow, or battery meter as small marks on the notice

Avoid:

- making one literal monster/body-creature the whole committee
- making it too medical
- treating the committee as evil; it is a real pressure/authority, not just an enemy

## Strong First Drawing Candidates

1. Around Here Chronicle newspaper front page
2. Monocle over a Chronicle clipping
3. Gazelle reporter
4. Biological Committee violation/reminder notice

These four form a clean cluster:

- place/jurisdiction has a newspaper
- newspaper has focused articles
- reporter gathers stories
- committees issue pressures and interruptions

## Committee For Comedic Standards

Role:

An enforcement/intervention body for jokes that have gone wrong: bad puns, dumb jokes, forced jokes, or jokes that need a penalty notice.

Symbol direction:

The cleanest symbol is probably not the whole committee. It is a served notice or fine delivered by a comedic representative carrying the wrong tool.

Possible visual anchors:

- official comedy violation notice
- fine or penalty slip
- clown serving paperwork
- mime serving paperwork, used carefully because the Mime also has a special silent-clarity role
- rubber chicken as corrective-action tool
- huge oversized mallet as emergency intervention tool
- stamp: `COMEDY VIOLATION`, `PUN FINE`, or `NOTICE SERVED`

Core joke:

The committee tries to mitigate a bad joke into a mainstream/funny joke with the wrong tool, and the fix does not work.

Avoid:

- making the committee simply "funny"
- treating the rubber chicken as Codex's tool
- making the wrong-tool intervention actually solve the joke too cleanly
- overusing the Mime and weakening the separate silent-clarity symbol
