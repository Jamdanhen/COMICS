## Production Workflow

Use a blocking-first, panel-by-panel workflow:

1. Define each panel's job.
2. Sketch or block each panel as its own readable cartoon.
3. Check continuity across panels.
4. Only then combine into one comic page prompt or composition.

After the page is blocked out, produce separate panel images rather than attempting the full comic page in one generation pass.

Reason:

This comic has too many concept shifts to safely solve all at once:

- office setting
- Razorforge introduction
- anatomy chart
- symbolic anatomy
- mine-habitat diagram
- blob living inside habitat
- escape through grate

Separate panel work should reduce drift, preserve each beat, and make continuity easier to correct before final assembly.

Working communication rule:

When a panel direction has already been decided, make the brief, store it, and move to the next panel. Do not show prompt text or process scaffolding to Taskmaster unless Taskmaster asks to read or revise it.

## Current Six-Panel Spine

Panel 1:

Arrival / problem.

Taskmaster and the escaped idea blob in the office or workspace. Razorforge is introduced by arrival, attention, or analytical presence rather than a sit-down meeting.

Panel 2:

Anatomy chart appears.

Razorforge presents or unfolds an old-style anatomy chart for the idea blob.

Panel 3:

Close-up symbolic anatomy.

The chart shows the anatomy / physiology of the idea through symbols rather than ordinary explanatory labels.

Potential symbolic organs:

- eye cluster
- fragile preservation jar
- noodle bowl or noodle uncertainty
- duplicate mini-blobs
- absurdity token
- grid or classification fragment
- mine-layer hint
- sealed unknown crate

Panel 4:

Mine-habitat design chart.

Razorforge designs an environment where the idea can live and be studied. The official concept is the Mine, but the visual grammar can borrow from a complex hamster habitat: chambers, tunnels, observation points, feeding/rest zones, exercise loop, air shaft, and a tempting side grate.

Panel 5:

Blob inhabits mine-habitat.

The idea blob has moved into the designed mine habitat. It grows and fills the space. Tiny hearts may show that it likes the house. Any hamster-wheel gag should remain minor/background if used.

Panel 6:

Grate squeeze escape.

The blob presses through a sewer-grate-like barred tunnel outlet. Razorforge-style notations, measurement marks, or symbols may appear around the rim. The habitat worked, but the idea is already exceeding the designed structure.

## Boundary

Do not include Codex in this comic.

This page is about Razorforge's method, not Codex's later machine.

The page should end with the idea understood and partially housed, but still changing and pushing beyond structure.
