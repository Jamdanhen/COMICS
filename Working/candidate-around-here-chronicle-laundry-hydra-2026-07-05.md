```text

Laundry Hydra / Full-Cycle Textile Containment

FORMAT

Create a black-and-white newspaper-style comic prop from Around Here.

THE AROUND HERE CHRONICLE

- black-and-white newspaper comic language
- simple readable line art
- dry institutional tone
- straight-faced absurdity
- low word count where possible
- no color
- no realistic rendering
- no full comic panel sequence

CORE PREMISE

A domestic observation has been classified as a tabletop-role-playing-style monster theory.

Laundry behaves like a regenerating household encounter.

It is not merely a pile of clothes.

It is a recurring textile adversary with delayed spawn rules, hidden dependencies, and incomplete-cycle regeneration.

The only known way to temporarily defeat it is the full-cycle containment method:

- strip the bed linens
- take a shower
- include dirty towels
- remove all currently worn clothing
- wash every remaining textile load
- prevent any unwashed textile from remaining in active domestic circulation

This creates a brief but logically valid state in which the Laundry Hydra has no legal regeneration target.

MAIN CHRONICLE HEADLINE

Naked Man Misidentified While Attempting Full-Cycle Laundry Hydra Containment

POSSIBLE SUBHEAD

Witnesses Mistake Mathematically Complete Household Optimization For Public Disturbance

ARTICLE ANGLE

The joke is not that the article knows it is ridiculous.

The joke is that the newspaper recognizes the logic of the procedure while the outside world misclassifies it.

Outside classification:

"naked man babbling about laundry hydra"

Around Here classification:

Household Systems Theorist
Incident: Attempted full-cycle textile eradication
Adversary: Laundry Hydra
Status: Misunderstood during valid anti-regeneration procedure

THE MONOCLE COLUMN

Column title:

The Monocle

Focus headline:

A Closer Look at the Anti-Regeneration Laundry Hypothesis

Column function:

The Monocle is the focused analysis column.
It does not merely report the incident.
It examines whether the underlying claim has merit.

Primary question:

Was he wrong?

Core hypothesis:

Laundry cannot regenerate if no unwashed textile remains in active domestic circulation.

MONOCLE ANALYSIS POINTS

The hypothesis has merit because laundry often regenerates from overlooked textile categories.

Known regeneration sources:

- bed linens
- bath towels
- currently worn clothing
- work clothes needed before Monday
- socks
- secondary towels
- forgotten hamper contents
- chair-draped garments
- "not dirty enough yet" clothing

The full-cycle method attempts to remove every active textile exception at once.

This creates a temporary zero-laundry condition.

Unresolved complications:

- doorbell risk remains under-modeled
- dryer delay may create exposure windows
- sock teleportation requires further study
- bed linens must be included or the containment ritual is incomplete
- unexpected visitors may trigger social-combat side effects
- final-load timing creates concentration checks
- clean-body state is required to avoid recontamination

TABLETOP ENCOUNTER LANGUAGE

Laundry Hydra

Type:
Regenerating household encounter

Traits:
Delayed Spawn
Hidden Textile Dependency
Hamper Recursion
Sock Displacement
Monday Pressure Aura

Lair actions:

At initiative count 20, bedding generates a new load.

At initiative count 10, towels become heavier than expected.

At the end of the round, one sock vanishes unless paired and secured.

Anti-Regeneration Clause:

Laundry cannot regain hit points if no worn textiles remain within 30 feet of the creature.

Containment Vulnerability:

If an unexpected visitor appears during the final-load ritual, the visitor must make a Wisdom save.

On failure:
The visitor suffers immediate social confusion and backs away.

On success:
The visitor still leaves, but retains the haunted knowledge that they interrupted a valid household optimization.

Important framing:

The subject is not the hazard.

The subject is the terrain condition.
```
