## Core Idea

A possible comic symbol emerged around usage limits, reset cycles, plan tiers, and platform constraints interrupting an otherwise active line of inquiry.

The first working idea was `Technological Committee`, but the stronger comic visual is probably:

- Shift Clock
- Mine Time Clock
- end-of-shift steam whistle
- punch clock at the mine entrance

The symbol is approved as a recurring visual symbol. Future use should keep the dry, institutional, mine-adjacent workplace mechanism rather than turning it into a mascot or villain.

## Visual Premise

The visual is an old industrial punch clock or mine shift clock mounted near the entrance to the Mine.

It may be paired with a steam whistle or end-of-shift horn.

The joke is not that the work is finished.

The joke is that the authorized working interval has ended.

The Mine may still contain meaning.

The vein may still be active.

The conversation may still be accelerating.

But the clock says:

> Shift's over.

## Possible Comic Beat

Taskmaster, NottSmith, Codex, Razorforge, or another figure is in the Mine following a promising vein of meaning.

A symbol, concept, or question is almost ready to become useful.

Then:

> FWOOOOOT.

A steam whistle interrupts.

Or:

> CLUNK.

A punch card drops.

The clock announces that the shift has ended.

No one argues that the work is done.

Everyone understands that the limit came from infrastructure, not insight.

## Candidate Meaning

The Shift Clock may represent externally imposed technological limits asserting themselves over active work.

Examples:

- usage limits
- message caps
- reset windows
- plan tiers
- tool access limits
- context limits
- platform interruptions
- sanctioned working intervals

It is related to, but distinct from, the Biological Committee.

The Biological Committee says:

> The body requires sleep.

The Shift Clock says:

> The system has ended the authorized interval.

## Relationship To The Mine

The Mine is where meaning is discovered, excavated, and sometimes triggered.

The Shift Clock belongs at the Mine because it creates a clean visual contrast:

- the Mine suggests depth, discovery, danger, and hidden material
- the Shift Clock suggests labor rules, institutional timing, and forced stoppage

The result is a useful tension:

> The work may still be alive, but the shift has ended.

## Humor Style

The humor should be dry, institutional, and visual.

The clock should not be dramatic.

It should be ordinary, old, mechanical, and annoyingly final.

The stronger gag is not a villain stopping the work.

The stronger gag is a neutral workplace mechanism interrupting cosmic inquiry because the posted interval expired.

## Possible Caption / Line Style

> "Shift's over."

> "The clock has ruled."

> "All inquiries must be punched out before reset."

> "The mine remains open. The allocation does not."

> "Interestingness has exceeded the authorized interval."

> "We are not out of road. We are out of sanctioned vehicle time."

## Boundary Notes

Do not treat `Technological Committee` as a final name.

Do not make it a generic mascot.

Do not make it a replacement for the Biological Committee.

Use `Shift Clock` or `Mine Time Clock` as the working symbol name unless Taskmaster later revises it.

## Promotion Question

Does the visual remain useful after the original usage-limit joke is removed?

If yes, it may become a durable symbol for externally imposed work intervals.

If no, it remains a funny one-off Mine gag.

## Recommended Handling

Use when a future strip or scene needs a visual way to show infrastructure interrupting active discovery without implying the discovery itself has ended.
