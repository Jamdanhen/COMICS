## Preserved Source Statement

Taskmaster represents the user.

The user has an executive director level job involving the balancing and management of a human service system. The work involves people being supported in their homes, jobs, and broader lives through a network of providers.

The role is not simple command. It involves guidance, oversight, training, standards, conformity, and system-level coordination across a provider network.

Systems are a major way the user creates consistency, clarity, and standards across that larger real-world support system.

## Campaign Style Source

Taskmaster's campaign style is improvisational.

The campaign often begins from a rough idea or sketch.

At the table, Taskmaster improvises details, preserves player agency, and responds to player actions.

Some details are roughed out only lightly at first. Some become significant later because of player choices, later interpretation, or newly discovered importance.

Examples include:

- The hard drive.
- Hubcap.
- Other details that gain significance across sessions.

This is a central source of the comic symbol system: things may begin as uncertain, improvised, partial, or low-emphasis details, then later become important.

## Information-Containment Pressure

After Session 000 and Session 001, it became clear that the campaign was producing too much information to contain casually.

The process evolved from:

1. Handwritten notes.
2. Transcription and dictation.
3. Campaign log structures.
4. Using transcription to fill campaign logs.
5. Asking whether the information could be extracted more systematically.
6. Developing candidate structures for better extraction and organization.
7. Building toward a database that can extract from dialogue and manage campaign information.

## Project Scope

The project is no longer only about organizing one role-playing campaign.

The campaign is a test environment for a broader methodology.

The longer endpoint is a transferable information-management method that could apply across disciplines:

- Role-playing campaign management.
- Other game masters' campaign processes.
- Business and professional workflow.
- Taskmaster's real-world executive director work.
- Potentially broader methodology or consulting applications.

## Behavioral Significance

The comic should understand Taskmaster's systems orientation as grounded in real responsibility, not abstract bureaucracy.

Taskmaster creates systems because systems are how complex human networks become understandable, consistent, guided, and usable.

The project tension is therefore not merely "too much information."

The deeper tension is:

- Improvisational creation generates living complexity.
- Living complexity creates containment pressure.
- Containment pressure requires systems.
- Systems must preserve meaning without killing agency, uncertainty, humor, or discovery.

## Boundary

This source material should inform Taskmaster's character and the comic's process logic.
