## Core Clarification

This note is about the conceptual geography and landscape of Around Here.

It is not the mythic BEAM concept.

The Around Here map idea is not literal geography and not a single town.

It is a growing jurisdiction with districts:

- some settled
- some still being excavated
- some rumored
- some mapped only by stories

## Emotional Geography

The landscape feeling comes partly from Pennsylvania:

- old mining towns
- railroads
- hidden valleys
- places that were once thriving, then half-forgotten
- old railroad grades
- stone foundations
- collapsed tunnels
- forgotten bridges

The point is not to copy Pennsylvania literally.

The point is the feeling that individual remnants imply a larger forgotten structure.

## Lost City Clarification

The Lost City is not simply on the map.

It is what the map gradually becomes.

One road does not reveal the city.

Many accumulated roads, walls, artifacts, foundations, tunnels, keystones, and relationships eventually cause the realization:

```text
Wait...
We've been standing in the outskirts of a city this whole time.
```
