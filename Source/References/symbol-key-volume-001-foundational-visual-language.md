## Revision Notes After Review

Taskmaster liked most of the iconography but identified two corrections:

- The warning that labels and symbols are subject to change without warning should remain a footer/joke warning, not become a numbered symbol panel.
- The Beam icon should not be represented as connected dots or a graph-like relationship diagram. That does not carry the Stephen King / Dark Tower connotation of reality-supporting Beams. If that idea cannot be shown clearly yet, remove the secondary mythic function from Volume 1 and define a better Beam icon later.

Volume 1 should be revised before being treated as promoted/locked source material.

## Versioning Joke

Approved concept:

The volume/version numbering of the visual language documents can become part of the running joke.

Examples:

- Volume 1 v2
- Volume 2A
- Volume 2B
- Volume 3, provisional
- Revision subject to change without warning

This supports the comic's larger theme that the language evolves through iteration, correction, and reclassification.

Future titling rule:

`Volume 1` and `Volume 2` are the core title anchors. The end of the title or script marker is where the versioning joke should live.

Examples:

- `Volume 2A`
- `Volume 2A.1`
- `Volume 2A, Revision B`
- `Volume 2, Appendix A-ish`
- `Volume 2A, Now With Fewer Unapproved Beams`

Use the suffix as the joke while keeping the core volume number legible.

## Beam / BEAM Typographic Rule

Ordinary `beam` means structural support or potential lever.

Mythic `BEAM` means the rare high-significance, reality-supporting concept associated with gunslinger mythology.

Use all caps and bold visual treatment for mythic `BEAM` when possible.

The distinction can become a joke because a character can say "BEAM" but cannot verbally show bold, caps, italics, or highlighting.
