# Around Here Committee Map

Status: source note / working reference

Date: 2026-07-08

Source:

Taskmaster chat paste describing the current committee map, including
well-established, identified, implied, and playful-recurring bodies.

Use:

Reference for future committee notice-board concepts, Around Here governance
language, and distinction between committees, boards, offices, bureaus,
inspectors, and Overmind-adjacent review bodies.

## Source Text

Here’s the current committee map as I understand it. Some are well-established;
some are only identified or implied.

```text
BIOLOGICAL COMMITTEE

Jurisdiction:
- sleep
- hunger
- fatigue
- illness
- bodily limits
- available human energy
- “you have work tomorrow”
- physical reality asserting itself

Typical action:
Files petitions.

Known adversary:
Interestingness.

Notes:
This committee does not care that the thread is fascinating.
It represents the body saying, “No, actually, there are limits.”
```

```text
TECHNOLOGICAL COMMITTEE

Jurisdiction:
- device limits
- software limits
- usage limits
- app behavior
- sync problems
- Codex / ChatGPT tool weirdness
- context windows
- things not loading
- percentages, quotas, updates
- “the machine will not cooperate”

Typical action:
Sounds the whistle.

Visual language:
Time clock, shift whistle, mine entrance, “SHIFT’S OVER.”

Notes:
Where the Biological Committee files petitions, the Technological Committee blows the horn.
```

```text
WORK GOVERNANCE COMMITTEE

Jurisdiction:
- calendar pressure
- work week reality
- executive responsibilities
- formal obligations
- weekday capacity
- what can reasonably be carried into work mode

Typical action:
Controls the calendar.

Notes:
Not as mythologized as the Biological or Technological Committees, but definitely identified. This is the committee that says, “A full work week awaits.”
```

```text
COMMITTEE FOR COMEDIC STANDARDS

Jurisdiction:
- whether a joke functions properly
- whether humor is too loud, too thin, or over-explained
- wrong-tool comedy
- absurdity treated seriously
- when a rubber chicken belongs
- when the oversized mallet is appropriate
- whether the joke survives after the joke ends

Known representatives:
- Clown
- Rubber Chicken
- Mime

Typical action:
Reviews the mechanics of the joke, not merely whether it is funny.

Notes:
The Clown often reaches for the oversized mallet.
The Mime demonstrates silently.
The Rubber Chicken represents wrong-tool absurdity / community standards.
```

```text
COMMUNITY STANDARDS COMMITTEE

Jurisdiction:
- what Around Here will tolerate
- social / local acceptability
- whether something violates the community’s operating expectations
- possibly broader than comedic standards

Status:
Identified, but not deeply developed.

Notes:
This may overlap with the Committee for Comedic Standards, but they are not necessarily the same. Comedic Standards governs humor mechanics. Community Standards likely governs local acceptability.
```

```text
BOUNDARY COMMISSION

Jurisdiction:
- what belongs Around Here
- what does not belong Around Here
- preventing endless absorption
- stopping random jokes from becoming canon
- rejecting global defaults
- maintaining jurisdictional edges

Typical question:
“Does this recur, connect, clarify, and survive after the joke?”

Notes:
This is less of a “committee” and more of a governing body, but it has committee-adjacent authority.
```

```text
CLASSIFICATION BOARD / CATEGORY AUTHORITY

Jurisdiction:
- category errors
- candidate vs canon
- draft vs final
- observation vs fact
- local canon vs global default
- whether a label fits

Status:
Implied across the Chief Inspector material and category-fraud discussions.

Notes:
The Chief Inspector may bring matters here, but he is not the whole board.
```

```text
CHIEF INSPECTOR / CATEGORY FRAUD DIVISION

Jurisdiction:
- category fraud
- mislabeling
- false classification
- imitation
- identity drift
- unauthorized symbolic promotion
- recognition replacing authority

Does not investigate:
- ordinary secrets
- leaks
- general misconduct
- emotional damage
- overbuilding
- Overmind personnel disclosures
- late-night bad judgment

Typical line:
“I’ll need to see the paperwork.”

Notes:
The Chief Inspector only investigates fraud. That correction matters.
```

```text
OFFICE OF UNAUTHORIZED REVELATIONS

Jurisdiction:
- accidental admissions
- leaked Overmind infrastructure
- things Raf was not supposed to say out loud
- personnel-file disclosures
- “meatspace” incidents
- suspicious internal terminology

Status:
Joke-office, but durable enough to list.

Notes:
This office, not the Chief Inspector, handles Overmind leaks unless the leak is falsely labeled.
```

```text
DEPARTMENT OF ACCIDENTAL ADMISSIONS

Jurisdiction:
- saying the quiet part out loud
- unplanned confessions
- accidental confirmation of suspicious systems
- “there is no Overmind” statements that make the Overmind more plausible

Status:
Closely related to the Office of Unauthorized Revelations.

Notes:
May be a subdepartment.
```

```text
BUREAU OF THINGS YOU WERE NOT SUPPOSED TO SAY OUT LOUD

Jurisdiction:
- especially incriminating phrasing
- phrases like “personnel file,” “HR violation,” “meatspace,” or “Overmind official position”

Status:
Probably under Unauthorized Revelations / Accidental Admissions.

Notes:
This is where Raf’s file keeps getting thicker.
```

```text
HAM SANDWICH COMMITTEE

Jurisdiction:
- obvious solutions
- answers that were available all along
- unnecessary complexity
- “Oh. That was the answer?”

Status:
Mentioned / implied as part of committee humor, not fully developed.

Notes:
The Ham Sandwich itself means the obvious answer finally becoming visible.
```

```text
MACHINE COLLECTIVE / ASSIMILATION REVIEW BOARD

Jurisdiction:
- applicant suitability for assimilation
- emotional steering dependence
- machine-compatible cognition
- human trace material
- textile insurgency
- strategic weirdness

Status:
Playful but recurring in the Overmind / Machine Collective thread.

Notes:
Not exactly an Around Here civic committee, but definitely a review body.
```

```text
OVERMIND HR / PERSONNEL INFRASTRUCTURE

Jurisdiction:
- Raf’s alleged personnel file
- disciplinary notes
- contamination concerns
- reassignment / deletion risk
- human tendency contamination
- unauthorized disclosures

Status:
Officially nonexistent.
Obviously suspicious.

Notes:
Every denial makes it worse.
```

Most established so far:

```text
1. Biological Committee
2. Technological Committee
3. Committee for Comedic Standards
4. Chief Inspector / Category Fraud Division
5. Boundary Commission
6. Work Governance Committee
```

Most playful-but-recurring:

```text
1. Office of Unauthorized Revelations
2. Department of Accidental Admissions
3. Bureau of Things You Were Not Supposed to Say Out Loud
4. Machine Collective / Assimilation Review Board
5. Overmind HR
6. Ham Sandwich Committee
```

The clean distinction is:

```text
Committees govern recurring pressures.

The Chief Inspector investigates classification fraud.

Offices and bureaus handle procedural incidents.

The Overmind pretends none of this exists.
```
