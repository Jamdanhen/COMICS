# Source Note - Committee For Comedic Standards

Status: source reference / not canon by itself

Source:

Taskmaster clarification in chat on 2026-06-19 while developing individual symbol language for future visual dictionary work.

## Clarification

The Committee for Comedic Standards issues fines and penalties when really bad puns, dumb jokes, or similar comedic offenses occur.

They typically serve notices through one of their members or representatives, such as:

- a clown
- a mime
- someone in a comedic role

The representative usually brings the wrong tool for the job, such as:

- a rubber chicken
- a huge oversized mallet

Sometimes the committee tries to mitigate a bad joke into what it perceives as a mainstream or funny joke using that wrong tool.

This attempt often does not work.

## Symbol Implication

The Committee for Comedic Standards is not simply "humor."

It is an enforcement and intervention body for jokes that have gone wrong.

Its notices, fines, penalties, and wrong-tool corrective actions may be more visually important than showing a full committee meeting.

## Existing Register Note

The current Committee Register treats the Mime as a special case that evolved beyond ordinary comedy committee representation into silent clarity. This source note does not erase that nuance; it preserves Taskmaster's clarification that mime-like comedic representatives can still belong to the committee's delivery language when appropriate.
