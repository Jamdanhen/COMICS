# Source Note - Dark Tower Beams / Breakers Fresh Sweep

Status: source reference / external mythology sweep

Date rebuilt: 2026-07-05

Reason rebuilt:

The prior root source file was deleted from the working tree. This file rebuilds the reference from a fresh sweep and focuses on content candidates for the Comics / Around Here mythic `BEAM` symbol.

## Scope Boundary

This is reference material for symbolic translation.

Do not reproduce Stephen King's specific protected imagery, characters, tower designs, named Guardians, scenes, or prose directly in final public comic art without a separate decision.

The goal is to understand what the Beams do and what visual cues can be translated into Around Here's black-and-white comic-symbol language.

## Strong Source Points

StephenKing.com glossary:

- The six Beams are like invisible high-tension wires.
- They cross at the nexus of the Dark Tower.
- They hold the Dark Tower in place.
- They maintain the integrity of time, space, size, and dimension.
- People can sense them when standing along their paths.
- Each end of a Beam is anchored by a Portal.
- The twelve Portals are overseen by animal Guardians.

Dark Tower Wiki / supporting reference:

- The Tower is held up by six Beams.
- The Beams are visible only by effects on the lands along their lines.
- One named effect is cloud patterns.
- The Crimson King seeks to destroy the Beams.
- Four Beams are destroyed before the Breakers are stopped.
- When the Bear-Turtle Beam begins to recover, the worlds may begin returning toward order and life.

Breakers:

- Breakers are powerful psychics used to weaken or break the Beams.
- They are imprisoned in Devar-Toi / Algul Siento in Thunderclap.
- They serve the Crimson King's effort to destroy the Tower by destroying its supports.
- The important Comics translation is indirect attack: the Beam is harmed through mental pressure applied to the structure, not by someone hitting a visible beam with a hammer.

## Core Translation

The mythic `BEAM` is not a visible object first.

It is:

```text
invisible cosmic tension
+ path
+ support
+ world-alignment
+ center relation
+ vulnerability to Breakers
```

The Tower is not the symbol by itself.

The Beam is the invisible structural path whose existence is proven by the world bending, flowing, or aligning around it.

## Content Candidates For Symbol Drafts

### 1. Cloud Corridor From The Spire

Visual:

A small distant spire at the bottom of a simple symbol square. Above it, clouds form a broad triangular or corridor-shaped shadow/path. The Beam itself is not drawn; clouds reveal it.

Meaning:

Invisible cosmic support made visible by cloud behavior.

Why it fits:

This uses the source cue that Beams are visible by effects such as cloud patterns. It also fits the user's uploaded visual reference: a sky corridor like a shadow or absence, but originating from a Tower spire rather than a mountain.

Risk:

Can become too scenic or poster-like if the horizon, tower, and clouds are over-rendered.

### 2. Invisible High-Tension Wire

Visual:

Two small terminal posts/portals with a barely indicated taut path between them. Clouds, birds, grasses, or small contour lines bend along the invisible line.

Meaning:

The Beam is a tensioned cosmic support that can be sensed by its effects.

Why it fits:

This comes directly from the official glossary's high-tension-wire analogy.

Risk:

May look too much like a mundane power line or cable unless the environmental effects carry the meaning.

### 3. Portal-To-Portal Path

Visual:

Two simple sealed portal markers at opposite ends, with a clear invisible corridor between them. The center is implied by a small empty circle or gap where the paths would cross.

Meaning:

Each Beam has anchored ends and crosses through the center.

Why it fits:

This translates the twelve portals / two endpoints per Beam structure without copying Guardian animals.

Risk:

Can drift back into diagram language if it becomes circles and connecting lines.

### 4. Cloud Pattern Plus Center Pull

Visual:

A compact sky symbol: several cloud bands all bend toward a small off-panel or edge-of-panel center point. The Beam is the empty aligned lane between them.

Meaning:

The world reveals the Beam by orienting toward the center.

Why it fits:

This may be the simplest dictionary-square version.

Risk:

Could read as weather unless the alignment is strong and intentional.

### 5. Breaker Stress Marks

Visual:

The cloud corridor is interrupted by small jagged pressure marks or psychic stress ripples. The Beam remains invisible, but the path shows strain.

Meaning:

The Beam can be weakened without being physically struck.

Why it fits:

This includes the Breakers concept while avoiding a character or full scene.

Risk:

Too many cracks or lightning marks may make it look like a storm symbol.

### 6. Recovering Beam

Visual:

A cloud corridor that was partly broken or distorted begins to smooth back into alignment. One side is chaotic; the other side resolves into a clean path.

Meaning:

When the Beam recovers, the world returns toward order.

Why it fits:

This uses the source idea that healing Beams may let worlds come back toward order and life.

Risk:

May be too complex for a first symbol.

## Current Best Direction

Best candidate for the comic's visual dictionary:

```text
Cloud Corridor From The Spire
```

Reason:

It preserves the mythic Dark Tower relation, uses the actual source visual clue of cloud patterns, and can fit Around Here's visual language if kept simple.

Recommended symbol form:

```text
small black spire
wide empty wedge / dark sky corridor
clouds bending along both sides
minimal horizon
no color
no landscape detail
no characters
```

The Beam should read as an absence or pressure path in the sky, not a drawn ray.

## Around Here Style Guardrails

Use:

- plain black-and-white comic-strip line art
- one readable visual idea
- object/effect based symbolism
- limited hatching
- no decorative occult geometry
- no compass/target diagram
- no full scenic painting

Avoid:

- tower poster
- fantasy logo
- mountains
- roads and towns
- literal steel beam
- rail grade
- sci-fi laser
- generic magic ray
- direct copyrighted Guardian animals or exact Dark Tower design
