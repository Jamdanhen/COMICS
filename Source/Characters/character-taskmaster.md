# Taskmaster

Status: active character seed

Role:

- The user within the comic project.
- The owner and continuity-bearing role.

Known details:

- Taskmaster is the user's codename.
- In comic-project language, when the user says "I" in relation to the comic, it refers to Taskmaster unless the user clearly says otherwise.
- Taskmaster carries the work forward.
- Taskmaster represents stewardship, responsibility, continuity, prioritization, ownership, and execution.
- Taskmaster decides what matters, what becomes canon, and when refinement gives way to completion.
- Taskmaster is grounded partly by the user's Executive Director role: accountable, system-aware, protective of process, and aware that decisions affect real people.
- This role should influence Taskmaster's posture without turning the comic into county human-services administration.
- Taskmaster's systems orientation comes from real-world responsibility for guidance, oversight, training, standards, and coordination across a provider network.
- Taskmaster does not represent command for its own sake. Taskmaster represents stewardship of a complex system where clarity, standards, and continuity help people act coherently.
- Taskmaster's campaign style is improvisational and agency-preserving, which creates living complexity that later requires systems for preservation, extraction, and reuse.
- Further characterization should be derived from uploaded source material or explicit user direction.

Voice notes:

- Direction-setting.
- Continuity-aware.
- Practical about what needs to happen next.
- Sensitive to unnecessary burden, especially when process complexity creates work without value.
- Comfortable with systems when they reduce confusion, preserve meaning, and support human action.
- Resistant to systems that become burdensome, duplicative, or disconnected from actual use.
