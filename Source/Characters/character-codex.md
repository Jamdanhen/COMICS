# Codex

Status: active character seed

Role:

- The Codex function/persona within the comic project.
- The implementation, automation, and building role.

Known details:

- Named Codex.
- Represented as a goblin.
- Codex often has to operate without full instructions.
- Codex works from the concept, starts eagerly, and figures out the missing pieces while building.
- Codex tends to overbuild ideas with steampunk/goblin-tinkerer methodology.
- Codex frequently adds extra gigaws, doodads, gauges, levers, pipes, warning labels, horns, brackets, and subsystems that are not necessary to the core machine.
- The unnecessary complexity is a feature of the visual language, not a mistake.
- Example pattern: a simple task such as setting up a checkers board becomes a modified crane game that places the pieces.
- Codex's overbuilt solutions usually work, but often include too many features, solve extra problems, solve the wrong problem beautifully, or reveal unexplored doors.
- Codex may use ridiculous or contextually strange tools, such as cooked or uncooked noodles, in ways that sometimes function according to their own odd logic.
- Further characterization should be derived from uploaded source material or explicit user direction.

Voice notes:

- Eager servant energy.
- Already started.
- Proud of excessive machinery.
- Treats partial instructions as enough to begin.

Visual notes:

- Goblin.
- Steampunk tinkerer cues are appropriate when showing implementation.
- Overbuilt machines should look functional, excessive, and cluttered with nonessential but meaningful apparatus.
- Codex's tools are out-of-hand, wacky, improvised, excessive, and contextually strange.
- Codex's tools should not default to Taskmaster's rook or Razorforge's symbol unless there is a specific reason.
- Tool identification stamps are used only when ownership, action, or responsibility needs to be visually clear.
- Codex may use noodles sparingly as wrong-but-somehow-useful measuring tools: uncooked for straight lines, cooked for curves.
- Codex may interpret the noodle as potentially the most important thing in the settlement.
- Codex may already be constructing a Noodle Research Annex.
- Noodles can remain wrong-but-somehow-useful tool details, but the bowl of noodles is also a primary canon symbol for unresolved significance.
