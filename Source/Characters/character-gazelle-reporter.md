## Core Role

The Gazelle Reporter is an anthropomorphic gazelle reporter for the Around Here Chronicle.

He comes from the abandoned first name `Around Here Gazette`, where the drift from `Gazette` to `Gazelle` became literal: a gazelle reporting the news.

He is a character, not just a symbol.

## Character Snapshot

The Gazelle Reporter is an older male correspondent with a serious, trusted broadcaster presence.

He should read closer to a veteran newsman than a young field reporter. His energy is composed, authoritative, observant, dry, and credible.

He reports in two modes:

- Chronicle / correspondence desk mode
- field reporter mode

## Visual Anchors

- anthropomorphic gazelle
- older male face
- calm intelligent eyes
- serious brow
- dignified long muzzle
- jacket when seated at the correspondence or news desk
- shirt and tie
- notebook, pencil, Chronicle bulletin, or update sheet
- press hat for field reporting only

## Wardrobe Rules

Desk / broadcast mode:

- must wear a jacket
- should read as a trusted correspondent
- no press hat

Field mode:

- may wear the press hat
- may carry notebook, pencil, satchel, or field notes
- should remain serious and professional, not over-eager

## Acting Range

Natural expressions and poses:

- steady news-update posture
- reading a Chronicle bulletin with sober concentration
- field reporting with notebook ready
- skeptical older-correspondent look
- restrained concern
- dry professional attention

Avoid broad emotion:

- goofy grin
- mascot cheerfulness
- young sidekick excitement
- theatrical outrage
- fashion-illustration posing

## Prop Logic

Props must be visibly held or used.

Notebook, pencil, papers, and bulletins should not float beside him as decoration.

When seated at the desk, his hands/hooves should rest naturally on papers, a bulletin, or the desk surface.

## Use Limits

Use him when the scene needs:

- Around Here Chronicle reporting
- news updates
- field observation
- institutional memory becoming public record
- serious reporting of unusual events

Do not use him as:

- a generic mascot
- a random animal joke
- a detective replacement for the Chief Inspector
- a comedy-standards representative
- a symbol-only icon when acting is needed

## Drift Risks

Avoid:

- making him too young
- making him cute
- making him sleek or fashion-model-like
- making the press hat his all-purpose look
- losing the Chronicle / Gazette origin
- turning him into a police or detective figure
- realistic animal rendering
