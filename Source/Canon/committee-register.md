## What Is A Committee?

A committee is not necessarily a group.

A committee is a recurring pressure.

A committee represents an external force, constraint, obligation, requirement, or influence acting upon the current activity.

Committees may be:

- Real
- Symbolic
- Internal
- External
- Biological
- Procedural
- Environmental

Most committees appear when attention, time, energy, or priorities are being pulled in competing directions.

## Committee Of Biological Standards

Primary function:

- Sleep
- Food
- Hydration
- Basic maintenance

Represents:

The reality that the operator is still a biological organism.

Typical messages:

- You should sleep.
- You should eat.
- You have been sitting here for six hours.
- You are running on caffeine and momentum.

Visual form:

- Cease and desist order
- Medical clipboard
- Alarm clock
- Bed

Visual differentiation note:

Committee notices are already part of the foundational visual language. The open design problem is how to distinguish a Biological Committee notice from a Committee of Comedy Standards notice without relying on too much label text.

## Committee Of Previously Answered Questions

Primary function:

Prevent redundant work.

Represents:

The frustration generated when an already-established conclusion must be repeatedly re-established.

Typical messages:

- You already answered this.
- This was resolved three reviews ago.
- See prior authority.

Visual form:

- Stacks of identical forms
- Duplicate questionnaires
- Filing cabinet that keeps printing the same page

## Committee Of Token Conservation

Primary function:

Resource preservation.

Represents:

- Artificial scarcity
- Consumption awareness
- Usage limits

Typical messages:

- You are running low.
- Would you like to buy more?
- Are you sure you wish to continue?

Visual form:

- Parking meter
- Fuel gauge
- Coin-operated gate

## Committee Of Mechanical Barricades

Primary function:

Technical interruption.

Represents:

- Software friction
- System errors
- Unexpected obstacles
- Interface failures

Typical messages:

- Error.
- Permission denied.
- Configuration required.

Visual form:

- Barricades
- Warning lights
- Locked doors

## Committee Of Excessive Refinement

Primary function:

Prevent completion.

Represents:

The temptation to continue improving something that is already functional.

Natural adversary of:

- Done Principle

Typical messages:

- One more revision.
- One more review.
- It could be better.

Visual form:

- Infinite staircase
- Mirror reflecting another mirror

## Committee Of Shiny New Projects

Primary function:

Redirect attention.

Represents:

- Interesting adjacent ideas
- Scope expansion
- Future work attempting to become present work

Typical messages:

- What if we also built...
- While we're here...
- Quick side project...

Visual form:

- Road signs pointing in every direction
- Unfinished construction

## Committee Of Unresolved Noodles

Primary function:

Maintain curiosity.

Represents:

Questions whose significance has not yet been determined.

Not hostile.

Often useful.

Typical messages:

- What about the noodle?
- Could that matter later?
- Are the Triads important?
- Was that a throwaway detail?

Visual form:

- Bowl of noodles
- Occasional noodle shop
- Question mark hanging from chopsticks

## Committee Of Campaign Continuity

Primary function:

Preserve connections.

Represents:

The desire to maintain coherence across people, locations, factions, events, clues, and consequences.

Typical messages:

- Wasn't this mentioned earlier?
- That connects to Session 2.
- What happened to Hubcap?

Visual form:

- String board
- Session logs
- Maps
- Roads

## Committee Of Discovery

Primary function:

Continue excavation.

Represents:

The belief that something valuable is still deeper in the mine.

Often correct.

Sometimes dangerous.

Typical messages:

- Keep digging.
- One more layer.
- We are almost there.

Visual form:

- Mine entrance
- Ore cart
- Pickaxe

## Committee Of Done

Primary function:

Counterbalance discovery.

Represents:

The understanding that value is created by completion as well as exploration.

Natural adversary of:

- Committee Of Excessive Refinement

Typical messages:

- Ship it.
- This is sufficient.
- Done is good enough.

Visual form:

- Stamped approval
- Completed bridge
- Finished comic

## Committee Of Comedy Standards

Representative:

- The Clown

Primary tools:

- Rubber Chicken
- Oversized Mallet
- Wrong Tool For The Job

Primary function:

- Humor
- Absurdity
- Perspective
- Quality control for jokes

Represents:

The refusal to take everything completely seriously.

The Committee of Comedy Standards rarely solves problems correctly.

It frequently solves the wrong problem.

Surprisingly, this occasionally works.

Secondary function:

Comedic enforcement.

The Committee of Comedy Standards is responsible for identifying and responding to comedic crimes, especially:

- Bad jokes
- Forced jokes
- Overexplained jokes
- Labor-intensive jokes
- Excessively complicated punchlines
- Puns that should have remained hypothetical

Typical notices:

- That joke was not approved.
- Please explain why this is funny.
- That pun caused injuries.
- Comedy permit denied.
- We regret to inform you that the joke has become work.

Severity levels:

- Smirk: acceptable.
- Chuckle: good.
- Laugh: excellent.
- Groan: committee review required.
- Audible sigh: formal warning issued.
- Rubber chicken deployment: corrective action.
- Oversized mallet deployment: emergency intervention.
- Comedic cease and desist: immediate termination of joke.

Visual form:

- Rubber chicken
- Oversized wooden mallet
- Tiny clown car
- Improvised solution
- Official notice stamped: `THIS JOKE REQUIRES REVISION`

Visual differentiation note:

Comedy Standards notices may need a distinct flavor from Biological Committee notices through delivery method, wrong-tool props, stamp language, border style, or representative presence. This distinction is open for brainstorming.

## Special Case: The Mime

The Mime was originally associated with the Committee of Comedy Standards.

However, the Mime proved unexpectedly useful.

Instead of serving as comic relief, the Mime briefly integrated into the crew.

The Mime evolved into a symbol of:

- Silent clarity
- Direct communication
- Information requiring no explanation

Because of this evolution, the Mime is no longer considered a standard representative of the Committee of Comedy Standards.

The Mime became its own symbol.

Usage:

Rare.

Very rare.

Overuse destroys value.

Observation:

- The clown represents the wrong tool applied enthusiastically.
- The mime represents no tool applied correctly.

## Committee Authority

The Committee of Comedy Standards does not determine whether a joke is funny.

It determines whether the joke has become so painful that intervention is required.

When a joke crosses that threshold, a notice is issued.

When multiple notices are ignored, the rubber chicken appears.

When the rubber chicken fails, the oversized mallet is authorized.

This process is considered best practice.

## Mature Interpretation

The committees evolved.

Initially they were perceived as obstacles.

Later they became stakeholders.

The mature interpretation:

Every committee represents a legitimate pressure.

The problem is not that committees exist.

The problem is determining which committee currently has authority.

## Final Note

Committees are strongest when symbolic.

A committee should rarely appear as an actual meeting.

A committee notice is usually more effective than a committee member.

The clown is usually more effective than the committee.

The mime is usually more effective than the clown.

All committees remain subject to interpretation, revision, promotion, and retirement without warning.
