## Classification

Around Here is a jurisdiction.

It is not:

- a location
- a city
- a state of mind
- geography
- a nation
- a building
- a town
- a literal map location

A person, object, question, contradiction, or symbol can be Around Here without physically being anywhere specific.

## Definition

Around Here is the region where:

- observations become symbols
- questions become stories
- contradictions are preserved
- meaning accumulates
- significance emerges

Final short definition:

> Around Here is the place where observations are preserved long enough to become mythology.

## Title Candidate

Around Here is a candidate for promotion as the comic's title.

Reason:

The concept may be broad enough to hold the comic's mythology, symbols, residents, committees, unresolved questions, and source-origin logic.

Status:

Candidate only until Taskmaster explicitly approves it as the title.

## Function

Primary function:

Around Here answers the question:

> "Where did this come from?"

The answer is:

> "Around Here."

Secondary function:

Around Here is where things are allowed to remain parked until a better framework appears.

Questions may remain open.

Contradictions may remain unresolved.

Classification may be deferred.

No penalty is assessed.

## Around Here Law

Law #1:

When something becomes sufficiently interesting, the Biological Committee loses authority.

Law #2:

Interestingness outranks authority.

Law #3:

If an observation survives long enough, it becomes a symbol.

## Icon Guidance

Around Here needs a sign.

The sign matters because every mythology eventually needs a `You Are Here` marker.

The joke is that the marker does not help anyone find their position. It only confirms that they have already arrived.

Potential visual forms:

- road sign
- town sign
- welcome sign
- map marker

The sign should appear ordinary.

It may be:

- hand-painted
- weathered
- slightly incorrect
- directional but unhelpful

The sign should imply:

> You have arrived.

Without specifying where.
