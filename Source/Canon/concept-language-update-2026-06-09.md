## Approved Concepts

### Review Spiral

The Review Spiral is usually a tempting, helpful process that gets out of hand.

It can occasionally become a villainous trap if someone or something tries to pull the project into review without progress.

### Mine

The Mine is a growing conceptual labyrinth inside the original mountain of project development.

It contains roads, branches, shafts, tunnels, connections, ore, and artifacts.

The Mine does not simply receive questions. It expands through exploration of concepts and methods.

### Ore

Ore represents raw ideas or potential discovered in the Mine.

When the Mine context is active, raw ideas may appear as literal rocks or ore.

### Crucible / Lost City

The Crucible is the distant endpoint city of the project.

It represents the possibility that a method created for extracting useful structure from raw campaign material could transfer first to other game masters, then later to Taskmaster's professional workflow, and potentially into a marketable methodology or consulting endpoint.

The Crucible is approved as a high-level mythic endpoint, but its visual form remains open.

### Rook Origin

The Rook is tied to chess, board-game strategy, stewardship, anchoring, and the chessboard as a structured strategic space.

### Committees

Committees are recurring biological, mechanical, review, inspection, and interruption forces.

They may include tiredness, hunger, token limits, dying computers, and review bodies such as the Committee for Comedic Standards.

### Rubber Chicken

The Rubber Chicken is a wrong-tool symbol associated with the Committee for Comedic Standards or similar committees.

It is not primarily part of Codex's toolkit.

### Codex Unusual Tools

Codex may use ridiculous or contextually strange tools, such as cooked or uncooked noodles, in ways that sometimes function according to their own odd logic.

### Codex Overbuild

Codex's overbuilt solutions usually work, but often include too many features, solve extra problems, solve the wrong problem beautifully, or reveal unexplored doors.

### Razorforge Refinement

Razorforge cuts away excess, compresses concepts, translates ideas, and asks hammering questions that shape the idea.

### Taskmaster Authority

Taskmaster is the final authority in all projects, including the comic project.

The visual sign of that authority is not yet identified.

Taskmaster's authority is grounded partly in the user's Executive Director role. This should appear as stewardship, accountability, system awareness, practical prioritization, and sensitivity to real consequences.

This is character grounding, not a recurring literal subject, unless Taskmaster explicitly asks.

### Beam

The Beam is a rare high-significance symbol.

It may sometimes draw from gunslinger mythology.

Do not overuse it.

Typographic distinction:

- Ordinary `beam` can refer to a structural support.
- Mythic `BEAM` should be written in all caps and, when possible, visually bolder than surrounding text.
- This distinction can itself become a joke because spoken dialogue cannot show bold, caps, italics, or highlighting.
- The mythic BEAM icon remains deferred until a non-confounding visual form is developed.

## Noodle Use Clarification

Status: superseded by dedicated canon file

See:

`C:\comics\Source\Canon\noodle-bowl-canon.md`

The Noodle Bowl is now the primary canon symbol for unresolved significance:

- Potential significance
- Potential irrelevance
- Unknown

The noodle shop is a derivative location or extension of the symbol.

The noodle bowl may appear frequently without explanation. Its appearance does not require dialogue, commentary, acknowledgment, or interpretation.

Codex may still use noodles sparingly as detail or measuring-gag material.

Approved uses:

- An uncooked noodle as a tiny straightedge.
- A cooked noodle as a curved measuring line.
- Codex holding a noodle like a precision instrument while normal tools are ignored.
- A noodle tucked among Codex's tools as a wrong-but-somehow-useful detail.

Boundary:

The noodle bowl may sit near a process as unresolved significance, but should not automatically be treated as a required process step.
