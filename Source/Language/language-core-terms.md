# Core Language Terms

Status: seed glossary

## Approved Terms

Taskmaster:

- The user.
- The user's codename and core self-referential role.
- In comic-project notes, "I" usually means Taskmaster when the user is describing comic action, perspective, or identity.
- Stewardship, ownership, continuity, prioritization, and execution.

Razorforge:

- ChatGPT.
- The ChatGPT function/persona in the comic project.
- May also be described as an interpretive AI or processing function when reader clarity requires it.
- Interpretation, extraction, compression, pattern recognition, synthesis, and refinement.
- Razorforge's own symbol belongs on Razorforge's face/head and notebook.
- Razorforge's tool symbols are used when needed to show that Razorforge is fixing, tweaking, interpreting, or refining something.

Codex:

- The Codex function/persona in the comic project, represented as a goblin.
- Implementation, automation, building, maintenance, and experimentation.
- Often works from partial instruction and overbuilds the concept.
- Codex's tools are wacky, improvised, and excessive rather than cleanly symbol-branded.

Tool identification stamp:

- A symbol placed on a tool or object when the scene needs to clarify who is acting, fixing, tweaking, interpreting, approving, or carrying responsibility.
- Not every tool needs to be branded.
- Process-significant objects, such as Razorforge's notebook, may carry identity symbols more consistently.

Around Here:

- Core symbol.
- Classification: jurisdiction.
- Not a literal location, city, building, nation, geography, or state of mind.
- The place where observations are preserved long enough to become mythology.
- The region where observations become symbols, questions become stories, contradictions are preserved, meaning accumulates, and significance emerges.
- Answers the question: "Where did this come from?" with "Around Here."
- Allows questions, contradictions, and unresolved classifications to remain parked until a better framework appears.
- Icon guidance: an ordinary sign, road sign, town sign, welcome sign, or map marker that confirms arrival without identifying a useful physical location.
- The sign joke: every mythology needs a `You Are Here` marker, but Around Here's marker does not help locate position; it only confirms arrival.

Around Here Chronicle:

- Official newspaper of Around Here.
- Records events occurring within the jurisdiction of Around Here.

Around Here law:

- When something becomes sufficiently interesting, the Biological Committee loses authority.
- Interestingness outranks authority.
- If an observation survives long enough, it becomes a symbol.

Noodle:

- Short form: Shape is the symbol. The noodle is a potential catalyst. A noodle becomes a noodle when someone notices it.
- Shape-based symbol for unresolved significance and potential catalyst.
- Potential significance, potential irrelevance, unknown, or nothing at all.
- Funny because it refuses classification.
- A noodle becomes a noodle when someone notices it, not when someone explains it.
- The noodle is not unknowable; it refuses premature classification.
- Observe first, classify later.
- Originated from a Shadowrun player questioning whether an earlier campaign element mattered or was throwaway detail.
- The bowl of noodles is the primary canon symbol.
- The noodle shop is a derivative location or extension of the symbol.
- Audience should encounter the noodle before understanding it: mystery first, language later.
- Shape is the symbol; shape is noodle language.
- Usage boundary: noodles do not begin appearing in comics, prompts, panels, props, or generated images until Taskmaster explicitly says to introduce them.
- The bowl may appear frequently without explanation, commentary, or acknowledgment.
- It provides continuity, curiosity, ambiguity, patience, and possibility.
- Codex may still use noodles sparingly as measuring-gag tools.
- An uncooked noodle may act as a tiny straightedge.
- A cooked noodle may act as a curved measuring line.
- A noodle bowl should not automatically become part of the main process flow; it can sit nearby as unresolved significance.

Noodle shape variants:

- Straight spaghetti: Practical Noodle; utility, measurement, direct solution.
- Rotini: Spiral Noodle; recursion, death spiral, excessive complexity.
- Dough: Potential Noodle; not yet formed, contains future possibilities.
- Box labeled "NOODLES": Classified Noodle; unknown contents and unknown significance.
- Absurd Noodle: wrong noodle, wrong application, wrong tool; the mismatch is the joke.

Gigaws and doodads:

- Extra nonessential mechanisms or decorations that signal overbuilt complexity.
- Useful for showing Codex's eager goblin-tinkerer methodology.

Overbuilt machine:

- A functional but unnecessarily elaborate implementation.
- Visual shorthand for Codex involvement.

Rook:

- Core symbol tied to Taskmaster, structure, framework, order, civilized construction, and strategic anchoring.
- Related concepts include repository, organization, architecture, chess, board-game strategy, stewardship, and the chessboard as structured strategic space.
- Taskmaster's tools and personal objects may use the rook when ownership, authority, stewardship, or action needs to be visually clear.

Compression and extraction:

- The repeated process of reducing raw material into relationships, concepts, symbols, and usable comic structure.

Archive:

- Durable project memory.
- Symbolic source of truth once material is approved or promoted.
- Contrasts with temporary chats, interfaces, scaffolds, and generated drafts.

Review Spiral:

- Recursive review that creates more review without moving toward a decision.
- Stopped by identifying the smallest useful next action.

beam / BEAM:

- `beam`: ordinary structural support or potential lever.
- `BEAM`: rare mythic load-bearing reality/support concept, sometimes drawing from gunslinger mythology.
- Write mythic BEAM in all caps and visually bold when possible.
- The formatting distinction may become a joke because spoken words cannot show bold, caps, italics, or highlighting.
- The final mythic BEAM icon is deferred for later visual development.

Fragile Detail Jar:

- A visual symbol for preserving useful uncertain details without promoting them to canon or forcing an immediate decision.

One True Intake:

- The principle that information should enter once and then be derived, reconciled, validated, or reused everywhere.

No-Write Boundary:

- A limit showing that approval, staging, or review does not automatically authorize durable archive/repository changes.

Human-Facing Control Panel:

- A process or interface that Taskmaster can actually use without doing machine work, decoding raw paths, or copying burdensome blocks.

Mine:

- A growing conceptual labyrinth inside the original mountain of project development.
- Contains roads, branches, shafts, tunnels, connections, ore, and artifacts.
- Expands through exploration of concepts and methods.
- Does not always produce rules, signs, procedures, or clean answers.
- May include dead ends, wandering paths, hazards, cliffs, unstable tunnels, spiral pits, overlooked veins, and discoveries that only later reveal what kind of structure is needed.
- Sometimes the value of the Mine is finding the place where a rule, role sign, procedure, warning, or preservation tool is needed, rather than producing the tool immediately.

Crucible:

- The distant lost-city endpoint of the project.
- Represents transfer of the extraction methodology from campaign work to other game masters, then later to professional workflow and potentially a marketable consulting methodology.

Committees:

- Recurring biological, mechanical, review, inspection, and interruption forces.
- May include tiredness, hunger, token limits, dying computers, and review bodies such as the Committee for Comedic Standards.
- A committee is not necessarily a group; it is a recurring pressure acting on the current activity.
- Committees may be real, symbolic, internal, external, biological, procedural, or environmental.
- Mature interpretation: committees are legitimate pressures, and the problem is determining which committee currently has authority.

Rubber Chicken:

- A wrong-tool symbol associated with the Committee for Comedic Standards or similar committees.
- Not primarily part of Codex's toolkit.

Committee Notice:

- Usually more effective than showing an actual committee meeting.
- Represents a pressure, interruption, review, warning, or competing authority.

Committee of Comedy Standards:

- Handles humor, absurdity, perspective, joke quality control, and comedic enforcement.
- Uses rubber chicken, oversized mallet, wrong tools, tiny clown car, and notices.
- Intervenes when a joke becomes painful, overexplained, forced, labor-intensive, or excessively complicated.

Clown:

- Representative of the Committee of Comedy Standards.
- Wrong tool applied enthusiastically.

Mime:

- Rare symbol of silent clarity, direct communication, and information requiring no explanation.
- No tool applied correctly.
- Use very rarely; overuse destroys value.

Reflection On The Reflection:

- Comic concept where the same artifact is reviewed by different committees or characters.
- Each reviewer may produce a different but valid interpretation.
- Key principle: the observation often says more about the observer than the artifact.
- Final insight: sometimes the discovery is not what changed, but who was looking.
