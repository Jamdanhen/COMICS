## Series Function

This page introduces Razorforge through method rather than through a formal meeting.

Razorforge's method is:

- observe the escaped idea
- make the idea legible
- diagram its anatomy
- design an environment where it can live and be studied

The page should show that Razorforge's interpretation is useful, careful, and meaningful, but not final containment. The idea remains alive and continues to find ways to grow through the system.

## Page Rules

- No Codex.
- No formal sit-down meeting.
- No business-conference staging.
- No full polished rendering until rough blocking is accepted for the next production stage.
- The idea blob stays small, cute, and cartoon-like in the office panels.
- Avoid true horror.
- Avoid making the idea look evil.
- Avoid treating Razorforge's structure as punishment or imprisonment.
- Use symbols and tokens as anatomy language, not heavy labels.
- The ending is not a full escape. It is a sly "found a way to grow" beat.

## Six-Panel Script

### Panel 1 - Arrival / Problem

Taskmaster is in the office/workspace near the desk and computer.

The idea blob is on the desk, small and cartoon-like, roughly computer-screen scale.

Razorforge is already beside the workspace, focused on the blob with notebook or pointer in hand.

Razorforge is introduced through attention and competence rather than entrance drama.

Panel meaning:

The problem is shared now. The idea is not just loose; it is something to understand.

### Panel 2 - Anatomy Chart Appears

Razorforge presents a physical old-style anatomy chart of the idea.

The real small blob remains nearby on the desk and looks toward the chart.

Taskmaster watches the process.

Panel meaning:

Razorforge's first move is not containment. It is analysis.

### Panel 3 - Symbolic Anatomy Close-Up

Close on the anatomy chart.

The idea is shown like a strange specimen, with internal structures and small symbolic organs.

Symbols may include:

- eye cluster / attention
- fragile preservation jar
- noodle bowl or noodle uncertainty
- duplicate mini-blobs
- grid/classification fragment
- mine-layer hint
- sealed unknown crate
- small absurdity or obscure-meaning token

The real small blob is in the foreground looking at the chart.

Panel gag:

The blob recognizes itself: "oh, that's me" without words.

Panel meaning:

The idea has anatomy. Some parts can be recognized; some parts can only be preserved or represented.

### Panel 4 - Mine-Habitat Design

Razorforge presents the second chart: a mine-habitat plan.

The design is a serious old structural mine plan, not a pet cage and not a machine.

It includes:

- rooms/chambers
- tunnels
- support structures
- observation space
- functional zones
- a visible drain or side outlet marked as part of the route

The hamster-habitat influence is only readability grammar: small livable zones, enrichment logic, and routes.

Panel meaning:

Razorforge is designing an environment where the idea can live and be studied.

### Panel 5 - Blob Inhabits The Mine-Habitat

The mine-habitat plan becomes a symbolic environment.

The small blob has moved into the rooms and tunnels.

It likes the environment. Tiny hearts may appear.

The drain or outlet is visible, but the blob has not found or used it yet.

The blob begins to grow into the structure without overwhelming the whole page.

Panel meaning:

The habitat works. The idea can live there. The problem is that living things grow.

### Panel 6 - Found A Way To Grow

Close on the same drain or barred outlet from Panel 5.

The blob has found the route.

One eye or soft pseudopod presses through the bars or opening.

Razorforge-style notations or measurement marks surround the rim.

Panel meaning:

This is not a catastrophic escape. It is the same kind of sly final beat as the computer-screen eye from the previous page: the idea found a way to grow through the structure.
