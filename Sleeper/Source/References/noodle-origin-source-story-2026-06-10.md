# Noodle Origin Source Story - 2026-06-10

Status: source story

Source:

Taskmaster chat explanation, 2026-06-10.

## Preserved Story

In an early Shadowrun session, the player character The Chin had Russian mob ties.

A job came down through those ties: watch for Triad activity.

At that stage, the Triads were buying or investing in property in the Grayline Docks area. They had a stake in a warehouse and were opening a noodle shop and a tattoo parlor.

At the table, those details initially functioned like current leads and setting flavor. They were unnamed locations and not immediately revisited.

Several sessions later, after real-world time had passed between game sessions, The Chin's player texted Taskmaster to ask whether the Triad reference had been a throwaway detail.

Taskmaster's answer was simply:

`No.`

No further explanation was given.

That one-word answer became important because the player was clearly still thinking about the unresolved thread away from the table. The behavioral interpretation became: somewhere, a player was thinking about his character, with that single word echoing without elaboration.

In comic-symbol terms, that transported into the image of The Chin standing in front of a noodle shop, wondering whether it mattered.

That became the symbol for unanswered questions.

The noodle later gained another layer when an early comic draft containing noodles was shown to a coworker, who asked:

`What's with the noodles?`

That moment repeated the same uncertainty at a different boundary: reality touching the imagined form and producing the same question state.

## Source Meaning

The noodle is not only an unresolved campaign detail.

It is the feeling of:

- A detail that may matter.
- A detail that has not been revisited.
- A restrained answer that confirms relevance without revealing why.
- A player continuing to think after the session ends.
- GM knowledge and player knowledge staying deliberately separated.
- A real person encountering the comic symbol and recreating the uncertainty by asking what it means.

## Boundary

The full campaign explanation should not be exposed casually in the comic because the underlying material may include GM knowledge versus player knowledge.

The comic can preserve the feeling and symbolic structure without retelling the campaign plot.
