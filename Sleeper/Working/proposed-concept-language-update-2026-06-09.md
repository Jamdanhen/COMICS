Identify which newly described concepts should be adopted into the comic project language now, which should remain deferred, and which need follow-up questions.

## Recommended Adopt Now

### Review Spiral

Adopt as:

A tempting, helpful process that gets out of hand. Occasionally it can become a villainous trap when review prevents progress.

### Mine

Adopt as:

A growing conceptual labyrinth inside the original mountain of project development. It contains roads, branches, shafts, tunnels, connections, ore, and artifacts. The Mine does not simply receive questions; it expands through exploration of concepts and methods.

### Ore

Adopt as:

Raw ideas or potential discovered in the Mine, usually shown as literal rocks or ore when the Mine context is active.

### Crucible / Lost City

Adopt as:

The distant endpoint city of the project: a place where extracted method becomes transferable, usable by other game masters, later adaptable to professional human-services workflows, and eventually possibly marketable as a methodology or consulting endpoint.

Keep as high-level mythology until more visual rules are defined.

### Rook Origin

Adopt as:

The Rook is tied to chess, board-game strategy, stewardship, anchoring, and the chessboard as a structured strategic space.

### Committees

Adopt as:

Recurring biological, mechanical, review, inspection, and interruption forces. They may include tiredness, hunger, token limits, dying computers, and review bodies such as the Committee for Comedic Standards.

### Rubber Chicken

Adopt as:

A wrong-tool symbol associated with the Committee for Comedic Standards or similar committees. It is not primarily part of Codex's toolkit.

### Codex Unusual Tools

Adopt as:

Codex may use ridiculous or contextually strange tools, such as cooked or uncooked noodles, in ways that sometimes function according to their own odd logic.

### Codex Overbuild

Adopt as:

Codex's overbuilt solutions usually work, but often include too many features, solve extra problems, solve the wrong problem beautifully, or reveal unexplored doors.

### Razorforge Refinement

Adopt as:

Razorforge cuts away excess, compresses concepts, translates ideas, and asks hammering questions that shape the idea.

### Taskmaster Authority

Adopt as:

Taskmaster is the final authority in all projects, including the comic project. The visual sign of that authority is not yet identified.

### Beam

Adopt as:

A rare high-significance symbol. It may sometimes draw from gunslinger mythology. Do not overuse.

## Recommended Keep Deferred

### Archive Visual Form

Keep deferred.

The Archive concept is valid, but its final appearance can vary until locked down.

### No-Write Boundary

Keep deferred.

No final symbol yet.

### Human-Facing Control

Keep deferred.

Known requirement: easy to understand, not machine code.

Needs later discussion.

### Approved vs Draft

Keep deferred.

Still conceptual.
