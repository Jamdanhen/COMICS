# Information Creature Comparison - 2026-06-10

Status: draft comparison

Purpose:

Compare two possible visual treatments for information that outgrows its containers in the early comic concept about information capture and containment pressure.

## Candidate 1 - Gibbering Detail Mass

Image:

`C:\comics\Images\Generated\draft-gibbering-detail-mass-comparison-2026-06-10.png`

Concept:

A many-eyed, many-mouthed, many-limbed detail creature. It starts as one interesting detail, then sprouts player choices, NPC names, locations, unresolved threads, remembered details, rewards, contradictions, and source tags.

Use if the comic needs:

- Chaotic information pressure.
- Many competing details.
- Uncontained complexity.
- A visual reason Taskmaster needs a better process.

## Candidate 2 - Living Idea Blob

Image:

`C:\comics\Images\Generated\draft-living-idea-blob-comparison-2026-06-10.png`

Concept:

A living, mutating idea blob. It starts small and charming, then grows eyes, limbs, labels, hooks, and unresolved-detail tendrils as the process evolves.

Use if the comic needs:

- Sympathy for the information.
- A less monstrous visual.
- A stronger sense that the material is alive and valuable, not just chaotic.
- A clearer fit with the idea that the problem is containment, not evil.

## Candidate 3 - Living Idea Blob, No Mouth / No Labels

Image:

`C:\comics\Images\Generated\draft-living-idea-blob-no-mouth-no-labels-2026-06-10.png`

Concept:

A cleaner visual-only version of the Living Idea Blob. It removes the mouth and readable labels so the creature feels more symbolic, less explanatory, and less like it is speaking for itself.

Use if the comic needs:

- Information to feel alive without becoming a talking character.
- A more visual-symbolic creature with less exposition.
- A softer alternative to the Gibbering Detail Mass.
- A creature that can appear as recurring process pressure without stealing dialogue focus.

## Draft Boundary

Neither image is approved canon.

Both are comparison drafts for Taskmaster review.

## Source Prompt Notes

All images were generated as monochrome black ink comic concept illustrations for the private comic project.

The key instruction for both was that information should feel alive, useful, unruly, and increasingly difficult to contain.
